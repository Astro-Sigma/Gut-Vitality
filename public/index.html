Dietary Fiber: Your Gut's Best Friend

expand_more

Why it matters: Fiber feeds beneficial bacteria in your gut, helping them produce short-chain fatty acids that reduce inflammation and support overall health.

Daily Target: 25-38g

Top Fiber Sources:

yard Legumes 15g per cup
grain Whole Grains 6g per serving
eco Berries 8g per cup
emoji_nature Chia Seeds 10g per 2 tbsp
lightbulb

Pro Tip: Increase fiber gradually and drink plenty of water to avoid digestive discomfort.

Fermented Foods: Natural Probiotics

expand_more

Why it matters: Fermented foods contain live beneficial bacteria that colonize your gut and support digestive health, immunity, and even mental well-being.

Daily Target: 2-3 servings

Best Fermented Foods:

local_cafe Yogurt Live cultures
local_drink Kefir 12+ strains
restaurant_menu Kimchi Korean classic
food_bank Sauerkraut Raw, unpasteurized
coffee Kombucha Fermented tea
ramen_dining Miso Probiotic paste
lightbulb

Pro Tip: Look for "live and active cultures" on yogurt labels. Avoid heavily processed or pasteurized versions.

Hydration: Essential for Digestion

expand_more

Why it matters: Water helps fiber work properly, prevents constipation, and supports the mucus lining in your intestines that protects beneficial bacteria.

Daily Target: 64-100 oz

check_circle

Softens stool and prevents constipation

check_circle

Helps nutrients absorb through intestinal walls

check_circle

check_circle

lightbulb

Pro Tip: Drink water throughout the day, especially with high-fiber meals. Aim for pale yellow urine as a hydration indicator.
